01

Executive summary

What SkillRise is and why it matters

SkillRise is a mobile-first platform that integrates skill education, accredited certification, and local job placement into a single end-to-end experience. It is designed for everyday people in any neighborhood — especially those locked out of employment by lack of formal credentials, not lack of ability.

The platform removes three barriers simultaneously: access to affordable training, recognized proof of competency, and a direct pathway to employers who are actively hiring. By partnering with local businesses to co-design skill tracks, SkillRise ensures that every certified learner is job-ready from day one.

The core user experience is: pick a track → learn with your community → earn a verified certificate → get matched to local jobs and apply in one tap. No resume required to start. No degree gatekeeping. No long wait between completing training and finding work.

02

Problem statement

The gap SkillRise closes

For learners

Millions of adults are capable of skilled work but lack the credentials to prove it. Traditional education is expensive, time-consuming, and geographically inaccessible. Online courses exist in abundance but rarely connect learners to real jobs at the end. People complete certifications that employers have never heard of, or apply for jobs they're qualified for but can't get past a keyword filter.

For employers

Local businesses — especially SMEs in trades, logistics, healthcare support, hospitality, and tech — struggle to hire reliably. Platforms like Indeed flood them with unqualified applicants. They spend hours screening people who don't have the foundational skills they need. Many would prefer to hire locally and to train specific skills, but have no efficient way to do it.

For communities

Unemployment and underemployment create cascading social problems. When people can't access economic opportunity, neighborhoods decline. Existing workforce development programs are underfunded, bureaucratic, and rarely digital-first. There is no modern, mobile-native platform that treats skill-building as both a community and economic activity.

Users & stakeholders

Who the platform serves

L

Learner

Primary user. Adult seeking new skills and employment. May be unemployed, underemployed, or seeking a career change. Needs a mobile-first, low-friction experience.

E

Employer

Local business posting jobs and optionally co-designing skill tracks. Wants pre-vetted, job-ready candidates they can trust to meet a skills bar.

I

Instructor

Subject-matter expert who designs and delivers course content. May be an individual professional, trade body, or community college partner.

A

Platform admin

Internal team managing content quality, certification integrity, employer partnerships, and community health. Requires a back-office dashboard.

04

User journey

End-to-end learner flow

1

Discovery & onboarding

User downloads app, selects their neighborhood, and answers a short quiz about their goals and existing experience. The app recommends matched skill tracks.

2

Enroll in a skill track

User browses tracks, views job demand data and salary ranges for each, then enrolls. Tracks range from 2–8 weeks. Enrollment is free or low-cost.

3

Learn (with community)

Completes modules via video, interactive exercises, and quizzes. Optionally joins a local cohort group — neighbors in the same track can message, meet up, and study together.

4

Assessment & certification

Completes a final skills assessment (multiple choice + practical task submission). On passing, receives a verified digital certificate with a unique credential ID.

5

Job matching & application

App immediately shows matched open roles from local employers. User applies in one tap — their certificate and profile are sent automatically. Employers respond within the app.

6

Hired & ongoing growth

Once placed, user can continue to new skill tracks, level up existing ones, or mentor newcomers entering the same track they completed.

06

Non-functional requirements

How the system must perform

CategoryRequirement
PerformanceApp must load the home screen within 2 seconds on a 4G connection. Video lessons must begin playback within 3 seconds.
Offline accessAll downloaded modules must be fully accessible without a network connection. Progress syncs when connection is restored.
ScalabilityArchitecture must support 100,000 concurrent users in MVP and scale to 1M+ without infrastructure redesign.
AccessibilityAll screens must meet WCAG 2.1 AA. All video content must have captions. Text must be resizable up to 200% without loss of functionality.
SecurityAll user data encrypted at rest and in transit (AES-256 / TLS 1.3). PII handled in compliance with GDPR and CCPA. Certificates use cryptographic signing to prevent forgery.
Availability99.9% uptime SLA. Planned maintenance windows outside peak hours (02:00–04:00 local time).
LocalizationMVP supports English and Spanish. Architecture must allow additional language packs without code changes.
Data retentionCertificates and learning records retained indefinitely on request. Account deletion purges PII within 30 days.

08

Technology stack

Recommended implementation approach

Mobile

React Native

Single codebase for iOS and Android. Expo for rapid iteration. Offline support via SQLite.

Backend API

Node.js / Express

REST API with GraphQL layer for flexible querying. Hosted on AWS or GCP.

Database

PostgreSQL + Redis

Relational DB for core data. Redis for session management and real-time features.

Video delivery

Cloudflare Stream

Adaptive bitrate streaming. Cost-effective. Global CDN for low-latency delivery worldwide.

Certification

Open Badges 3.0

Industry-standard verifiable credential format. Blockchain-anchored hashes for tamper-proof verification.

Notifications

Firebase + Twilio

Push notifications via Firebase. SMS fallback for job alerts via Twilio.

Payments

Stripe

Employer subscription billing. Learner premium upgrades. Handles all regional tax compliance.

AI features

Anthropic Claude API

In-module study assistant, course recommendation engine, and job-profile matching logic.

09

Revenue model

How SkillRise generates income

Employer subscription

Monthly SaaS fee for businesses to post jobs and access certified candidate pool. Tiered by number of active listings.

Placement fee

One-time success fee charged to employer when a SkillRise-certified candidate is successfully hired and completes 30 days.

Premium learner plan

Free tier: 1 active track. Premium ($9.99/mo): unlimited tracks, priority job matching, and AI study assistant.

City & government contracts

B2G licensing to municipalities and workforce development agencies who subsidize access for unemployed residents.

Corporate training

Companies pay to train existing employees on new skill tracks, using SkillRise as an internal upskilling platform.

Course co-creation

Employers co-sponsor the development of specific skill tracks tailored to their hiring needs (featured branding in return).

Phased delivery plan

MVP to full product roadmap

Phase 1 — Months 1–3

MVP: One neighborhood, five skill tracks

Launch in a single city with 5 curated tracks (e.g. electrical basics, logistics, caregiving, commercial cooking, digital admin). Onboard 10 employer partners. Focus on learning flow, certification engine, and one-tap job application. Target: 500 enrolled learners, 50 certifications, 20 hires.

Phase 2 — Months 4–6

Expand: Community features + employer portal

Add cohort groups, in-app messaging, and employer dashboard. Expand to 3 cities and 15 skill tracks. Launch instructor portal so external SMEs can submit course content. Target: 5,000 learners, 300 hires.

Phase 3 — Months 7–12

Scale: B2G + AI personalization

Sign first municipal government contract. Launch AI study assistant and personalized track recommendations. Enable learner-to-mentor pathways. Expand to 10 cities, 40 skill tracks. Target: 50,000 learners, 5,000 hires.

Phase 4 — Year 2+

National rollout + corporate training

Open platform to any city or region. Launch corporate training product. Pursue international expansion starting with English-speaking markets. Target: 500,000 active learners.

Risks & mitigations

Known challenges and how to address them

High
Low course completion ratesMitigate with cohort accountability groups, push notification reminders, streak mechanics, and a completion guarantee (refund if you don't get a job interview within 90 days of certifying).
High
Employers don't trust the certificationMitigate by co-designing tracks with employers from day one. They set the skills bar. Build in a 90-day satisfaction guarantee — if a hire doesn't work out, next placement is free.
Medium
Cold start problem (no employers = no learners)Launch with employer partners pre-secured before public launch. Start in a single neighborhood so density is high. Offer first 6 months free to anchor employers.
Medium
Certificate fraud / impersonationRequire identity verification before certificate issuance. Use cryptographically signed credentials. Public verification URL lets employers confirm any certificate ID in seconds.
Medium
Content quality inconsistency across tracksAll instructor content goes through an editorial review process before publishing. Use learner ratings and completion data to surface and retire underperforming content.
Low
Regulatory compliance in vocational trainingPartner with accredited institutions to co-award certificates where local law requires it. Legal review in each new market before launch.
